Microsoft Office 365?

Office 365 is a cloud- based subscription service (hosted on external servers) that brings together all the services and tools most suited to new ways of working to help you grow your business.

Microsoft Office 365?

Office 365 for example allows all users to create and share documents from anywhere and any device (smartphone, tablet, computer), to benefit from a professional mailbox, instant messaging, create presentations, organize online meetings or create team sites.

Microsoft Office 365?

Using the power of the cloud and various applications, Office 365 meets the needs of mobility, real-time communication / collaboration, productivity and data security.

Tools of Microsoft office 360.

Office 365 includes, depending on the offer chosen, the most famous tools such as Word, Excel, Outlook, OneNote, Publisher, OneDrive for Business or Access. You probably know most of them; this is the reason why we will not dwell too much on these applications in this article. If you opt for Office 365, you will always have the most recent software versions and you won't have to worry about updates that will happen automatically.

OneDrive for Business

OneDrive for Business (equivalent to a Dropbox) is Microsoft's cloud computing service dedicated to professionals that allows you to store your files, share them with other people in the company and have access to them from any location. device (Android or iOS) and at any time. You also have the option of sharing files and data with people outside your company.
Unlike OneDrive, which is a free personal storage space , OneDrive for Business is perfectly suited to SMEs, mid-caps and large accounts.

SharePoint Online

SharePoint Online is one of the applications available in Office 365. Unlike SharePoint Server which is deployed on your own server, SharePoint Online is hosted in the cloud. This collaborative tool allows you to create team sites, an intranet or an extranet. It can also be used as an Electronic Document Management (EDM) tool. SharePoint Online is open to people outside the company wherever they are, which helps encourage collaborative work and communication with customers.

Exchange Online & Outlook

Exchange Online is the cloud-hosted mail server dedicated to professionals and the mail client to connect to is Outlook. Exchange Online therefore does not download, unlike Outlook. Note that it is quite possible to configure several email addresses . class="mb-5 ">By opting for Outlook, you will benefit from multiple functionalities: a professional inbox, a powerful calendar solution, a task list, a contact database, protection against spam , message encryption, email archiving, protection against spam. online threats, etc.

Skype for Business

Unlike the free version (the one everyone knows) which is perfectly suited for small businesses of less than 20 people, Skype for Business is ideal for SMEs, mid-cap companies and large accounts. This intuitive collaboration tool greatly facilitates interactions between company employees
Here are some of its features: online meetings (up to 250 participants) / video conferences, instant messaging, audio and video calls, desktop and file sharing, remote access.

Microsoft Teams

Officially launched in March 2017, Microsoft Teams is a team messaging tool that aims to provide an alternative to Slack . This collaborative platform is integrated into all Office 365 applications (such as Skype, Word, Excel, OneNote , SharePoint ...), which makes it a considerable advantage.
This tool allows for example to create private / public discussion threads by team , by project or by topic . For each discussion thread, it is possible to share documents, to chat of course or to receive notifications from other applications.
